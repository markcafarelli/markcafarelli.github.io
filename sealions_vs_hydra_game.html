🕵️ Observation Check: Genes and Alleles

Just like we've modeled genes using letters in Punnett squares, in this game we are modeling genes found on chromosomes using colors. Each different color represents a different allele (a version of a gene).

It doesn't matter what specific trait the color stands for—what matters is that the different colors visually show us genetic variation!

Look at the animations below. Notice how the Sea Lion offspring inherit different alleles (colored rings) than their parents, but the Hydra offspring has the exact same allele as its single parent. Keep this in mind as we begin the 10-year simulation!
